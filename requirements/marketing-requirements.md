## 1. Target Market and Segments

### Primary Market
Regulatory Affairs (RA) teams at life science companies that manage product registrations across multiple markets and health authorities. The core buyer is the Head or VP of Global Regulatory Affairs, while the primary daily users are RA Managers and RA Specialists.

**Total Addressable Market (TAM):**
There are approximately 6,000 pharmaceutical companies, 3,000 biotech companies, and 2,000 medical device companies globally with active regulatory portfolios. Not all require a dedicated RIMS — the serviceable addressable market is companies with 5+ active registrations across 2+ markets.

**Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):**
Estimated 2,500 organisations globally with 5+ multi-market registrations that currently lack a purpose-built RIMS. The majority of this segment uses spreadsheets and SharePoint today.

**Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) — Year 1:**
50–100 organisations. Initial focus on English-speaking markets (US, UK, Canada, Australia) and mid-size pharma/biotech (50–2,000 employees) where the decision cycle is shorter.

## 2. Competitive Landscape

### Competitor Analysis

#### 1. Veeva Vault RIM
**Description:** The dominant enterprise RIMS platform. Part of the Veeva Vault cloud suite. Used by large pharma and biotech.
**Strengths:** Feature-complete, deep eCTD publishing, established in large pharma, strong compliance record, extensive integration ecosystem.
**Weaknesses:** High cost (enterprise contracts, six-figure ARR), 6–18 month implementation timelines, complex for smaller teams, rigid UX not designed for mid-market.
**How RegAxis RIM Wins:** Faster time-to-value, self-serve onboarding, AI-native from day one, modern UX, fraction of the cost, no professional services required.

#### 2. IQVIA Regulatory Intelligence (Regulatory Tracker, iEnvision)
**Description:** IQVIA offers regulatory intelligence and submission tracking tools bundled with data services. Strong on intelligence feeds; weaker on operational workflow.
**Strengths:** Rich regulatory intelligence data, established relationships with large pharma, bundled with IQVIA data subscriptions.
**Weaknesses:** Data-subscription pricing model, not a workflow tool, limited self-service, requires IQVIA data contracts.
**How RegAxis RIM Wins:** Built-in AI intelligence without a separate data subscription, workflow-first design, open SaaS pricing.

#### 3. Lorenz / Extedo (eCTD Authoring Tools)
**Description:** eCTD authoring and publishing tools. Specialists in dossier structure and eCTD format compliance. Not full RIMS platforms.
**Strengths:** Deep eCTD format expertise, established with regulatory submissions teams, standalone desktop or server tools.
**Weaknesses:** Authoring-focused only; no registration tracking, renewals, analytics, or AI; not SaaS-native.
**How RegAxis RIM Wins:** End-to-end lifecycle management in one system; eCTD publishing is one module, not the whole product.

#### 4. PAREXEL Regulatory Track (and similar mid-market tools)
**Description:** Mid-market RIMS solutions with basic registration and submission tracking.
**Strengths:** More accessible than Veeva for mid-market.
**Weaknesses:** Aging UX, limited AI, minimal analytics, typically no dossier management integration.
**How RegAxis RIM Wins:** Modern AI-native interface, purpose-built analytics, dossier intelligence, better UX.

#### 5. Spreadsheet + SharePoint (incumbent non-software)
**Description:** The default "RIMS" for the majority of companies that lack a dedicated system. Excel for tracking, SharePoint/Teams for document storage.
**Strengths:** Zero cost, familiar.
**Weaknesses:** No audit trail, missed deadlines, no workflow, no analytics, manual effort intensive, not compliant with 21 CFR Part 11.
**How RegAxis RIM Wins:** Directly addresses every spreadsheet pain point: automated alerts, audit trail, structured workflows, AI assistance — at an accessible price point.

## 4. Key Messaging and Value Propositions

### Primary Headline
**"The AI-powered RIMS built for how regulatory teams actually work."**

### Supporting Value Propositions

**VP-01: Never miss a renewal deadline again.**
RegAxis RIM tracks every registration expiry date across every market and automatically alerts the right people at 180, 90, 60, 30, and 14 days. Your portfolio stays active; lapses become a thing of the past.

**VP-02: AI that works inside your regulatory workflow.**
From dossier gap detection to AI-drafted HA query responses with source citations, RegAxis RIM's AI features are embedded directly in the workflows where RA teams need them — not bolted on as a separate tool.

**VP-03: Audit-ready by design.**
Every record change — who made it, when, and what changed — is captured in a tamper-proof audit log that meets 21 CFR Part 11 expectations. Prepare for an FDA inspection with confidence.

**VP-04: From spreadsheet to structured system in hours, not months.**
Self-serve onboarding with CSV import. No professional services engagement required. Most teams have their portfolio entered and their first renewals tracked within a day.

**VP-05: Real-time visibility into your entire regulatory portfolio.**
One dashboard that shows every active registration, every open submission, every approaching deadline, and every AI-flagged risk — across all markets, all products, all in one place.

**VP-06: Priced for the mid-market.**
Per-seat SaaS pricing that scales with your team — no six-figure enterprise contracts, no hidden implementation fees.

### Secondary Messages by Persona

**For the RA Director:** "Get the portfolio visibility your team needs to brief the C-suite and stay ahead of risk — without chasing status updates."

**For the RA Specialist:** "Stop managing regulatory compliance in Excel. Spend your time on regulatory strategy, not spreadsheet maintenance."

**For the Submissions Manager:** "Coordinate multi-market submissions from one timeline view. AI helps you draft HA responses; the workflow ensures nothing falls through the cracks."

## 5. Go-To-Market Channels

### Phase 1: Awareness (Pre-Launch, Months 1–3)
- **Content Marketing:** Publish 2 articles per month on RegAxis.com targeting regulatory affairs professionals. Topics: "21 CFR Part 11 checklist for RA teams," "How to track pharmaceutical registrations without a RIMS," "Common submission mistakes and how to prevent them."
- **LinkedIn:** Company page with regular posts targeting RA Directors, Regulatory Affairs Managers, and Regulatory Operations leads. LinkedIn Sponsored Content for early access signups.
- **Industry Events:** Target DIA (Drug Information Association) Annual Meeting, TOPRA Annual Symposium, and RAPS Regulatory Convergence for booth/sponsorship presence.

### Phase 2: Early Access (Months 3–6)
- **Waitlist / Early Access Programme:** Accept 20–30 design partners from the waitlist. Offer 6 months free in exchange for structured feedback and case study participation.
- **Direct Outreach:** SDR-led outreach to RA Directors at mid-size pharma and biotech companies in the US, UK, and Canada.
- **Regulatory Affairs Communities:** Engage in RAPS forums, LinkedIn groups for regulatory professionals, and Slack communities.
- **Product Hunt:** Launch on Product Hunt to generate startup community awareness and early adopter signups.

### Phase 3: General Availability (Month 6+)
- **Self-Serve SaaS Funnel:** Free trial (14-day, no credit card) → in-app onboarding → paid plan activation.
- **SEO:** Target high-intent keywords: "RIMS software," "regulatory information management system," "pharmaceutical registration tracking software," "Veeva Vault alternative."
- **Partnerships:** Partner with regulatory consultancies (CROs, independent RA consultants) who can recommend RegAxis RIM to their clients.
- **Referral Programme:** Encourage existing customers to refer peer organisations with incentive (seat credits).
- **Webinars:** Monthly 45-minute webinars on regulatory compliance topics, with RegAxis RIM as the solution backdrop.

### Marketing Attribution Requirements

- All outbound email links must include UTM parameters (`utm_source`, `utm_medium`, `utm_campaign`, `utm_content`).
- All paid campaign landing pages must be A/B tested for headline and CTA copy.
- Attribution model: last-touch for initial analysis; linear multi-touch for campaigns above USD 5K spend.
- A marketing dashboard must aggregate acquisition data weekly and be available to the marketing and product team.

### Compliance for Analytics

- Cookie consent banner must be displayed to all EU visitors before any analytics pixels fire (GDPR).
- No personally identifiable information (name, email) may be sent to PostHog or GA4 event properties.
- User IDs sent to analytics must be anonymised/hashed.
